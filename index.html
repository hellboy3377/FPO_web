What are FPOs and SHGs?

FPOs (Farmer Producer Organizations) and SHGs (Self-Help Groups) are rural collectives that uplift communities through collaboration and entrepreneurship, particularly empowering women and farmers across India.

Self-Help Groups (SHGs)

Community-based organizations of 10-20 women who come together for mutual support, savings, and entrepreneurship development.

Farmer Producer Organizations

Collective business organizations of farmers that provide better market access, input procurement, and technology adoption.

Community Impact

Creating sustainable livelihoods, preserving traditional crafts, and building economic independence in rural communities.

Empowering Women Through Enterprise

Explore stories of impact, independence, and innovation across Odisha—from Sambalpur's handlooms to Koraput's organic millets.

Women Leadership

Empowering women to become entrepreneurs and community leaders through skill development and financial literacy.

Financial Inclusion

Providing access to credit, savings, and insurance services to support women's entrepreneurial ventures.

Skill Development

Training programs in traditional crafts, modern techniques, and business management for sustainable growth.
